RAG Architecture Guide

The RAG Stack: From Naive to Agentic

RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) grounds LLM (Large Language Model) responses in your own data. Each type trades off complexity, latency, and retrieval accuracy differently. — Hover nodes for details. Click cards to expand. Hit ▶ to trace the flow.

01 Naive RAG Vector Search

Retrieve → Generate. Fast to build, brittle in production. Falls apart when queries are ambiguous or data is complex.

Expand
02 Hybrid RAG Vector + Keyword

Semantic + Keyword search combined. Because sometimes exact words matter more than meaning. RRF or a reranker resolves conflicts.

Expand
03 Graph RAG Graph Traversal

Retrieves connected, relational context. Critical when your data has relationships that flat vectors can't capture. Underrated and underused.

Expand
04 Agentic RAG Agent-Driven

The model decides when and what to retrieve. Less pipeline, more reasoning. Where RAG is heading.

Expand
